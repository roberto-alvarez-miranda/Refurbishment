# Product Guidelines: Refurbishment

## Design System & Aesthetic
- **Theme:** "Industrial Modern" aesthetic, combining the reliability of engineering software with the refined usability of contemporary SaaS.
- **Color Palette:**
  - **Primary:** Deep Slate Blue (for structural UI elements like sidebars and headers).
  - **Accents:** Professional Blue (for interactive actions) and Construction Orange (used sparingly for warnings).
  - **Surfaces:** Tiered grayscale (white to light gray) for workspace backgrounds to reduce eye strain during data-heavy workflows.
- **Typography:** **Inter** font family.
  - *Crucial:* Use tabular (monospaced) lining figures for all numeric data (budgets, measurements) to ensure vertical alignment in tables.
- **Layout & Spacing:** 12-column fluid grid. Soft rounded corners (4px base) for standard elements, with high-density data tables (32px row heights).

## Prose & Tone
- **Voice:** Professional, reassuring, and precise. The tone should guide the user confidently through complex financial and structural decisions.
- **Clarity:** Avoid overly dense construction jargon where possible, or provide clear tooltips. Keep instructions concise.
- **Feedback:** Provide immediate and clear feedback on AI actions (e.g., when a blueprint is parsed or a budget is updated).

## UX Principles
- **Heads-up Display Approach:** The interface should act as a dashboard, prioritizing the visibility of the total budget and critical deviations at all times.
- **Data Density:** Maximize visible data in tables while using whitespace strategically to separate information clusters.
- **Progressive Disclosure:** Hide complex parametric settings behind "Advanced" toggles to avoid overwhelming non-expert homeowners.
